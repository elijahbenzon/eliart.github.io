BANGKOK, THAILAND

Introduction

Bangkok is one of the most beautiful and culture-rich cities of the world. Bangkok is the capital of Thailand, a country located in Southeast Asia bordering Myanmar, Laos, Combodia, and Malaysia. Whether the delicious and exotic food, the sparkling temples, or the night life, there are many places to see and activities to do. Without a doubt, this city remains of my favorite cities I have traveled to â€” here are some recommendations and some advice if you would like to travel to Bangkok!

Things To Do

There are countless activites to do and places to see in Bangkok. Want to chill and have a cup of coffee? Check out the city's cafes! Interested in Thailand's history and culture? Be sure to go to all the museums and temples! Enjoy shopping and outdoor music? Look for the many shopping malls and outdoor markets! There is something for everyone. Here are my favorite places to visit:

Temple of the Emerald Buddha

Known as the Wat Phra Si Rattana Satsadaram, this temple is the most sacred temple in Thailand. According to legend, the Emerald Buddha bring prosperity to the country it resides. This temple is filled with beautiful statues and buildings decorated with designs and jewels.
ICONSIAM Shopping Mall

This mall was built by the royal family. The mall is gigantic and stunning, and I recommend visiting the mall. The mall has an area where people cook thai food in front of you, such as in the picture. I recommend getting pad thai, coconut ice cream, and thai tea!
Temple of the Reclining Buddha

Known as Wat Phra Chetuphon Wimon Mangkhalaram Rajwaramahawihan or Wat Pho, this temple is near the Grand Palace. It houses the Reclining Buddha, which is 6 meters long and covered in gold leaf. You can walk around the statue as well.
The Grand Palace

This complex of buildings houses the Kings of Siam since 1782 and the king and his court until 1952. The Grand Palace is filled with magnificent buildings and a lot of sculptures and statues. Be sure to bring water as you will be walking around a lot!
Khlong Ton Sai District

This district is where the ICONSIAM Shopping Mall is located. There's a small ferry you can take to get across the river. The district is filled with beautiful statues and fountains, and at night, there are many festival lights for you to enjoy!
Siam Square

Definitely come here if you like outdoor markets and street food, fashion boutiques, small shops and cafes, music performances, or just the night life. This place always has something to do, whether in the day or at night!
Jim Thompson House Museum

Jim Thompson was an American businessman and architect who helped revitalise the Thai silk industry in the 1950s and 1960s. He suspiciously disappeared without a trace, which added to his popularity. His house was transformed into a museum where they give tours.
Siam Paragon

Siam Paragon one of my favorite malls in Bangkok. It is relatively close to other malls as well. Its filled with shops, artwork, and has a food market, where you can try Thai dishes and delicacies, other Asian cuisines, and of course, thai tea!
Pathum Wan District

I like this district because of the many art displays that are set up in the area. They also have street performers and music concerts for you to enjoy. Be sure to snap a picture by these sculptures!

Tips

With some idea on some places to visit in Bangkok, you will definitely need some advice on how maximize your experience in this wonderful city. Here are some tips for you to consider when traveling:

What is the best way to get around Bangkok?

Transportation depends on where you want to travel. Your main transportation to get around places will either be by taxi, the metro, or walking. You can rent bikes to travel around the city. Sometimes, you will need to go by boat or ferry to get across rivers.

Where are some places to stay in Bangkok?

  • Akara Hotel
  • Bangkok Oasis Hotel
  • Chatrium Hotel Riverside
  • Shangri-La Hotel
  • SilQ Boutique Hotel
Be sure to check out your travel agent to see which hotels fit you the best!

Since temples are sacred locations, are there any rules or regulations for tourists?

In most temples, you must cover your shoulders and legs, so make sure you are not wearing shorts or short skirts when you plan on visiting a temple. Additionally, you may be asked to take off your shoes when you enter a the temples containing buddha statues. Be sure to research before visiting a temple.
