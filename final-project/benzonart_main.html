Why Make Art?

This is a question I often hear, and honestly, it took me a while to fully answer it. As a child, everyone knew that I had some talent in drawing. From keeping a journal of sketches of animals as a child to practicing portraits in my middle school years to even winning some awards in high school, art was a part of my life. Then, why did I not decide to pursue art in university? The answer: I made art simply because I was good at it and everyone knew that.

My perspective changed while taking some art classes I took at the University of Miami. I learned that art can be a voice. Artists create art to display their passions, beliefs, and perspectives. In university, I explored different medias of art, while also searching for my voice. In my Identity Crisis series, I voice the societal-take on the idea of identity and incorporate personal experiences.

While the breath I make will continue to blossom as I further define myself as an artist, I hope my work will offer you new perspectives and inspirations. I hope to further my perspectives by opening to collaborations with other artists.
